MajorCycle — Transactional email templates

A single visual record of every email the app sends through Resend (Supabase auth emails are configured separately in Supabase and are not included here).

Source of truth: the code, not this file. These render from web/lib/email/* and web/app/(public)/contact/actions.ts. Every email shares one brand wrapper — renderBrandEmail() in brandEmail.ts (navy→blue gradient header, brand mark, disclaimer footer). The header logo below is the real email-icon.png embedded, exactly as production emails render it. Sample names, dates and addresses are illustrative.

Hi Alex,

We’ve received a request to delete your MajorCycle account (alex@example.com). It’s now scheduled for permanent deletion on Friday, 10 August 2026.

Until then, your account is deactivated but fully recoverable. To cancel the deletion, just sign back in any time before Friday, 10 August 2026 — everything picks up right where you left off.

Your subscription stays valid until the end of the period you’ve already paid for — deleting doesn’t cut it short or extend it. Sign back in before Friday, 10 August 2026 to keep your account; otherwise it’s removed then and won’t renew, so no further charges go out.

Sign in to cancel deletion

After Friday, 10 August 2026, your account, profile, and all associated data are permanently removed and can’t be restored.

If you didn’t request this, sign in now to cancel it and change your password.

© MajorCycle provides educational information only — not financial advice.
The 4th paragraph is status-aware. Shown above: paid subscriber. Other variants swap only that line:
· trial — “You’re on a free trial — it stays active until its normal end date, with no charge. Sign back in before [date] to keep your account; if the trial ends first, you’ll come back to a free account.”
· no subscription — the paragraph is omitted entirely.

Amount adapts to plan + currency (e.g. C$20, A$159). The “we’ll automatically retry” line shows only while Stripe Smart Retries is on. Sent once, on the first failed renewal — not on every retry, and not for a failed initial signup (Checkout handles that).
