Rover Scoring Methodology

Overall Rating (0–100)

A composite score that blends three pillars of the investment case into a single number designed to answer one question: is this stock worth my attention right now?

Overall Rating = (Financial Health × 0.40) + (Valuation Zone × 0.35) + (Momentum × 0.25)

Why these weights: Financial Health is given the largest weight because a great business at a fair price will outperform a fair business at a great price over a full cycle. Valuation Zone (where the stock sits relative to its historical drawdown band) is the timing input. Momentum is a tiebreaker that prevents catching a falling knife.

Health Score (0–100)

Measures the underlying financial strength of the business across three dimensions: profitability, balance sheet safety, and cash generation. Higher is better.

Health Score = (Profitability × 0.40) + (Balance Sheet × 0.35) + (Cash Generation × 0.25) Profitability = function(ROE, gross/op/net margin trends) Balance Sheet = function(D/E, current ratio, interest coverage) Cash Generation = function(FCF yield, FCF / net income, FCF trend)

Tiers: ≥85 exceptional · 70–84 solid · 50–69 adequate · <50 stressed.

Confidence Tier (Cycle Reliability)

The Verdict card's price bands depend on the number of distinct historical drawdown cycles the engine has detected for the stock. More cycles = larger statistical sample = more reliable Typical and Bound levels.

≥15 cycles → High confidence 10–14 → Solid confidence 5–9 → Moderate confidence <5 → Limited confidence — interpret with caution

Drawdown Band Math

Every price level on the Verdict card is derived from the trailing 252-day peak (back-solved from current price and current drawdown) and the historical cycle statistics:

peak = close ÷ (1 + drawdown/100) typicalPrice = peak × (1 + typ_dd/100) lowerBound = peak × (1 + lower_bound/100) band width = |lower_bound − typ_dd| ÷ 2 (half the distance to historical worst) invalidation = lowerBound × 0.95
